## Domain: Macroeconomics

### Benchmarks
- Real Business Cycle (RBC) as frictionless benchmark
- New Keynesian (NK) as the workhorse with nominal rigidities
- Heterogeneous Agent NK (HANK) as the current frontier
- Any new model should explain what it adds beyond these benchmarks

### Standard frictions and building blocks
- Nominal rigidities (prices, wages, or both)
- Incomplete markets / heterogeneous agents (uninsurable idiosyncratic risk)
- Bounded rationality / information frictions (rational inattention, level-k, diagnostic expectations)
- Financial frictions (collateral constraints, intermediary balance sheets)
- Search frictions (labor, housing, credit)
- One friction at a time for clarity; combining two requires strong justification

### Key areas
- **Monetary policy:** Transmission channels, forward guidance, zero lower bound, unconventional policy
- **Fiscal policy:** Multipliers, fiscal-monetary interaction, fiscal theory of the price level, debt sustainability
- **Business cycles:** Sources and propagation of fluctuations, heterogeneous exposure
- **Inequality and macro:** How aggregate shocks redistribute, how inequality affects aggregates
- **Expectations:** Departures from RE, belief formation, learning, sentiment

### What top journals expect
- Microfounded general equilibrium (partial equilibrium needs strong justification)
- Clear equilibrium concept (competitive equilibrium, recursive competitive equilibrium, Markov perfect)
- Welfare analysis when policy-relevant
- Connection to data: calibratable parameters, matchable moments, or testable predictions
- Key question: what mechanism/channel drives the result, and why does it matter for policy or measurement?
